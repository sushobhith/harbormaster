Cheat sheet
  • ⌂ row = one settlement. Chips = the hexes it collects from (colour = resource, number = dice roll).
  • Pips = the dots under a number = how often it rolls. More pips = more cards. That's your whole score.
  • road → = point that settlement's free road here.
  • ⚓ = you start on a port. Counts toward your total, so port players get slightly fewer pips. Still even.
  • ±N at the top = best player minus worst player. 0 or 1 = even game. Above 2 → hit Re-draw.
  • Weakest start plays first. Order is on each card.
Built-in rules
  • 6 and 8 never touch · twin numbers never touch · 2 never touches 12
  • No same-resource clumps · every resource gets a fair pip share
  • Ports fixed to match the real frame · land placed so a 2:1 port avoids its own resource
  • Harbors on the official scenario edges, types shuffled · 2:1 ports kept off their own resource
  • Settlement distance rule respected · 6/8 access evened across players
Seafarers · Heading for New Shores
  • Starting settlements go on the main island only — the island race is yours to play. Rules allow a ship instead of a starting road; the drawn road just shows which coast the plan valued.
  • Small islands use their own tile + number sets, including gold fields (gold = collect any resource).
  • First settlement on each small island = 2 bonus VP · play to 14 VP.
  • Robber: 4-player starts on the desert; 3-player has no desert — rulebook parks it on an island hills hex. Pirate starts on the marked sea hex.
Physical setup: lay tiles row by row as shown, tokens on top, robber on the desert. Ports are fixed to match the printed frame (photo-verified, both 4-player and 5-6 player) — rotate your frame so the port sequence lines up with the drawn board, then everything matches. Physical setup: assemble the Seafarers frame per the "Heading for New Shores" scenario diagram, then copy this board — the drawn shape, harbor positions, tile pools, and the two number-token sets all follow the official scenario; only the placement is balanced-random. Re-draw settlements keeps the board and only re-deals the starting spots and roads. Free and open source — view the code on GitHub.
FAQ

How is this different from other Catan board generators? Others balance the board. Harbormaster also deals each player two starting settlements, two roads, and a turn order, matched so the start-value gap is at most 1–2 pips. Prefer drafting? Board-only mode keeps the balanced board and leaves placement to the table.

Does it support Catan Seafarers? Yes — the Seafarers map generates the official "Heading for New Shores" scenario for 3–6 players: main island, small islands with gold fields, their two separate number-token sets, and harbors on the official scenario edges.

Can I share a board? Every board has a seed in the URL. Copy the link and everyone sees the identical setup, settlements included.

Is it free? Free, no signup, open source. One HTML file, runs entirely in your browser.
